1. The "Forge" (Where the task is done)
In a writing-centric app, the "Work" usually lives in a Manuscript Editor or a Journal Tab.

The Workflow: Bob flips a Vibe Card that says "Write a 500-word character sketch." * The Action: He clicks a button on the card that says "Start Writing."

The Transition: This takes him to a dedicated Manuscript Tab where a blank page (or a template) is waiting for him.

2. The "Submission & Validation" (The Judge)
This is the trickiest part. How do we know Bob actually did the work?

Option A (Self-Report/Honesty): Bob clicks "I did it," types a short reflection, and gets XP. (Fastest to build, but easy to game).

Option B (AI Peer Review): When Bob clicks "Submit," we send his manuscript and the Vibe Card's "Qualifications" to an AI agent. The AI checks if he met the criteria.

Success: "Ding!" XP Gained.

Failure: The AI gives feedback: "Great start, but you missed the 'Show, Don't Tell' requirement. Try again!" No XP yet.

Option C (Human Review): Jon (the Admin) sees a notification, reads the work, and clicks "Approve." (Best for quality, hardest to scale).
